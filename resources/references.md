# References and Resources

Textbooks (classic recommendations):

- Classical Mechanics — H. Goldstein, C. Poole, J. Safko
- Introduction to Electrodynamics — D.J. Griffiths
- Quantum Mechanics — J.J. Sakurai & J. Napolitano (or Griffiths for intro)
- Statistical Mechanics — R.K. Pathria & P.D. Beale (or K. Huang)
- Mathematical Methods — G.B. Arfken & H.J. Weber (or M. Abramowitz)

Online lecture series and notes:

- MIT OpenCourseWare (Classical Mechanics, Quantum Physics, Electrodynamics)
- Leonard Susskind Lectures (Stanford) — theoretical physics playlist

Software / tools:

- Python with NumPy, SciPy, SymPy, Matplotlib, Jupyter
- SageMath for symbolic computation (optional)

Useful links:

- [arXiv.org](https://arxiv.org) — preprints and lecture notes
- [Physics Stack Exchange](https://physics.stackexchange.com) — Q&A
